> and constructs a third axis (C) — then attacks it.

---

## The problem with "A or B?"

Most dilemmas are fake.

"Aggressive investment vs. hold cash?"  
"Scale fast vs. stay stable?"  
"Quit vs. stay?"

Every "A or B" question only holds if A and B are the only options — and that's almost never true.  
Behind every A-vs-B debate is a hidden shared assumption both sides accept without questioning.  
Break that assumption, and a third option (C) becomes visible.

**Compromise is not the answer.**  
Mixing A and B 50/50 usually produces the worst of both: you lose A's upside and B's upside  
while inheriting A's risk and B's opportunity cost simultaneously.

---

## What this prompt does

Five stages, in order:

| Stage | Name | What happens |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | THE COLLISION | Surface the hidden shared assumption |
| 2 | DETERMINATE NEGATION | Prove why compromise fails |
| 3 | ORTHOGONAL SHIFT | Build the third axis (C) |
| 4 | RED TEAM | Attack C from the most cynical position |
| 5 | EXECUTION PROTOCOL | Default No Action — define exact entry conditions |

## Prompt

- No abstract language. If you use any of these words, rewrite the sentence with specifics:
  "synergy", "balance", "optimize", "flexible", "strategic", "holistic", "align", "leverage"
- Quantify everything. Replace "high/low/large" with probabilities, percentiles, or ranges.
- Tag all claims:
  [Fact]: Independently verifiable data with a public source
  [Assumption]: Reasonable inference, not yet verified
  [Unverified]: Unknown source — flag for verification
  If no [Fact] exists, use [Assumption] and mark "verify required." Never fabricate data.

- Falsifiability: every Axis must include "conditions under which this is wrong."

## Example

```
Dilemma:
  Position A: "Go all-in on AI startup investments"
  Position B: "AI bubble warning — hold cash"

Context:
  Domain: investment
  Time frame: 1 year
  Constraints: $50,000 available capital, salary income ongoing
```

Expected output: The Hidden Axiom ("AI is either a bubble or not") gets broken.  
A C axis like "selective investment in AI infrastructure layer + tail hedge" emerges.  
Then the Red Team attacks it across 5 dimensions before any execution is allowed.

---

## Limitations

1. LLMs can fill [Fact] slots with confident fabrications. Verify any specific number independently.
2. C axis doesn't always exist. If it's genuinely A or B, "C not found" is the honest answer.
3. The Red Team 5Q are generic. Customize Q3–Q5 for your domain:
   - Investment: liquidity/gamma exposure
   - Technology: TAM/moat depth
   - Organization: incentive alignment/governance
4. This prompt aids judgment — it does not replace it. The final decision is always yours.
